FAQ

Common questions.

If we haven't answered yours, the fastest route is a quick WhatsApp message — we usually reply within an hour during clinic hours.

Ask on WhatsApp
Do I need to pay for the first consultation? +
Yes — we charge a flat ₹500 for a 30-minute consultation that includes a full clinical examination and review of any digital X-rays. This is adjusted against treatment if you proceed within thirty days.
Is dental treatment painful? +
Modern dentistry, done well, is largely painless. We use micro-injection techniques, topical numbing, and proper time to ensure you're truly comfortable before any procedure begins. For anxious patients we offer conscious sedation.
Do you accept insurance? +
We accept most major Indian insurance providers — Star Health, HDFC Ergo, Niva Bupa, Tata AIG, ICICI Lombard, Care Health, Aditya Birla, and Manipal Cigna. Cashless treatment is available for select procedures; we'll verify your coverage before treatment starts.
Can a root canal really be done in one sitting? +
For most cases, yes. We use rotary endodontic instruments and microscope-assisted technique to complete the procedure in a single visit. Severely infected cases occasionally require a second appointment for healing.
How long does a dental implant take? +
From start to final crown, most implant cases run 3 to 6 months — the bulk of which is healing time as the implant integrates with bone. Active treatment chair-time is usually 2 to 3 short visits.
What's your sterilization protocol? +
We follow CDC-aligned protocols — autoclave sterilization for all instruments, single-use disposables wherever possible, fresh barriers between patients, and a dedicated sterilization room separate from operatories.
Do you treat children? +
Yes — we see patients from age 3 onwards for routine care, fillings, and orthodontic evaluation. For complex pediatric cases requiring sedation we work with a trusted referral network.
What if I have a dental emergency? +
Call or WhatsApp us immediately on +91 72089 09768. During clinic hours we keep emergency slots open daily. After hours, send a message — Dr. Tanya often responds personally and will advise next steps.
