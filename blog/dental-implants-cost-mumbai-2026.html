What Is a Dental Implant — and What Are You Actually Paying For?

A dental implant is a titanium post surgically placed into the jawbone to replace the root of a missing tooth. Once it fuses with the bone (a process called osseointegration), a crown is attached on top, giving you a permanent, natural-looking tooth.

The total cost of a dental implant in Mumbai is made up of three components:

  • The implant fixture: the titanium screw placed surgically in the jaw
  • The abutment: the connector piece between the implant and the crown
  • The crown: the visible tooth, usually ceramic or zirconia

Some clinics quote only the implant fixture cost — which can make their price look far cheaper than it really is. Always ask for an all-inclusive quote covering all three components.

Single Tooth Implant Cost in Mumbai: What's Included?

For a single tooth implant at a reputable clinic in Andheri West or elsewhere in Mumbai, here is what a comprehensive package should include:

  • Initial consultation and clinical examination
  • Digital X-ray or OPG (panoramic jaw scan)
  • Implant placement surgery (titanium fixture)
  • Abutment fitting
  • Permanent ceramic or zirconia crown
  • Post-operative follow-up visits

What is not always included (and can add to the cost):

  • Bone grafting: required if there is insufficient jaw bone (adds ₹8,000–₹25,000)
  • Sinus lift: needed for upper jaw implants in certain cases (adds ₹15,000–₹40,000)
  • Temporary crown during the healing phase
  • CT scan / CBCT imaging if required

Full Mouth Implant Cost in Mumbai: All-on-4 vs All-on-6

For patients who have lost most or all of their teeth, implant-supported full-arch restorations (commonly called All-on-4 or All-on-6) are the gold standard alternative to traditional dentures.

All-on-4 (₹3,00,000 – ₹6,00,000)

Four implants are strategically placed to support a full arch of fixed teeth. The angled posterior implants often eliminate the need for bone grafting. Suitable for patients with moderate bone loss.

All-on-6 (₹4,50,000 – ₹8,00,000)

Six implants provide greater load distribution and stability. Preferred for patients with stronger bite force requirements or where additional implants improve long-term prognosis.

Zygomatic Implants (for Severe Bone Loss)

When patients have significant bone resorption in the upper jaw, zygomatic implants — anchored in the cheekbone rather than the jaw — offer a solution without the need for extensive bone grafting. Dr. Tanya Kusum is one of the few specialists in Mumbai trained in zygomatic implant placement.

Why Cheaper Isn't Always Better: What to Watch Out For

It is tempting to go with the lowest quote — but with dental implants, the long-term cost of a failed implant (requiring removal, bone grafting, and replacement) can far exceed the savings from choosing a cheaper option upfront.

Red flags to watch for:

  • Clinics that won't disclose the implant brand being used
  • All-inclusive packages that seem unusually low (under ₹15,000 total) — likely using unverified or generic implant systems
  • No pre-surgical CT scan or CBCT imaging
  • No mention of bone assessment or bone grafting evaluation
  • Treatment being performed by a general dentist rather than a trained oral surgeon or implantologist

The right questions to ask any clinic:

  • Which implant brand and system will you use?
  • Does the quoted price include the abutment and crown?
  • Will a CBCT scan be done before surgery?
  • What is your protocol if the implant fails?
  • What are your surgeon's qualifications?

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do dental implants last?

With proper care, implants can last 15–25 years or more. The titanium fixture itself is typically permanent; the crown may need replacement after 10–15 years.

Is the implant procedure painful?

The surgery is performed under local anaesthesia and is generally very well tolerated. Most patients report that it is considerably less uncomfortable than they expected. Mild soreness for 2–3 days post-surgery is normal.

How long is the full implant process?

From implant placement to final crown, the process typically takes 3–6 months to allow for osseointegration (bone fusion). Immediate-load options are available in certain cases, allowing a temporary tooth to be placed on the same day as surgery.

Can I get implants if I have bone loss?

Yes, in most cases. Bone grafting, sinus lifts, or zygomatic implants can be used depending on the degree of bone loss. Dr. Kusum will assess your bone volume using a 3D scan and recommend the most appropriate approach.

Does dental insurance cover implants in India?

Most standard individual health insurance plans in India exclude dental implants. Some corporate group policies include partial dental surgical cover — check your policy documents or HR department.
